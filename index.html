Reese's Peanut Butter Cups

History

Reese's Peanut Butter Cups were first introduced in 1928 by H.B. Reese, a former dairy farmer and shipping foreman for Milton S. Hershey. The popularity of the candy quickly grew and it has now become an iconic American chocolate treat.

Ingredients

Reese's Peanut Butter Cups consist of a chocolate cup filled with a creamy, sweet peanut butter. The chocolate cup is made of milk chocolate, while the peanut butter filling is made of sugar, vegetable oil, peanut butter, and other ingredients.
